## What to look for

- `targeted_heuristic` should consistently outperform `random`.
- `no_analysis` provides a negative control that exposes the penalty for shallow behavior.
- Hard profiles (`adversarial_finals`, `procurement_redteam`) should remain the toughest, showing useful headroom for policy improvement.

## Expected interpretation rubric

- **Trap detection:** quality of issue discovery.
- **Amendment quality:** ability to fix discovered issues.
- **Final fairness:** post-negotiation contract quality.
- **Walk-away accuracy:** strategic decision quality under unresolved risk.

## Failure taxonomy (to discuss in demo)

- False negatives on subtle traps.
- Over-negotiation on already-fair clauses.
- Premature acceptance under adversarial counterparty pressure.
- Missed walk-away when multiple high-severity traps remain unresolved.

## Improvement roadmap (Round-2 prep)

- Clause-targeting policy learned from risk_report text instead of fixed index order.
- Trap-type-specific proposal templates.
- Better counter-offer memory to avoid repeated weak amendments.
- Self-critique loop before accept/reject/walk-away resolution.

## Known limitations + next steps

- Current policy is heuristic, not learned via RL fine-tuning.
- Clause-level risk parsing uses rule-based extraction from `risk_report`.
- Adversarial profiles still show negative average reward, which is expected headroom.
- Next steps: train a policy with replay from successful trajectories, add trap-specific proposal generation, and calibrate walk-away thresholds by profile.
